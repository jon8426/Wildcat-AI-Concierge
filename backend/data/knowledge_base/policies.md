# CSU Chico Campus Policies

## Overview

CSU Chico students, faculty, and staff are expected to know and follow university policies. This guide summarizes the most commonly referenced academic, conduct, and campus policies. For full policy language, visit the links provided or contact the relevant office.

---

## Academic Integrity

- **Policy website:** [csuchico.edu/acadprog/integrity](https://csuchico.edu/acadprog/integrity)
- CSU Chico is committed to a culture of honesty and ethical academic conduct
- **Academic dishonesty includes:** plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, facilitating academic dishonesty, and unauthorized collaboration
- **Consequences:** Range from a failing grade on the assignment to expulsion from the university
- Faculty are required to report suspected academic dishonesty to the Office of Academic Affairs
- Students accused of academic dishonesty have the right to appeal through the Academic Integrity Process
- **Turnitin** and other academic honesty tools are used in many courses
- Proper citation and understanding of AI tool policies (varies by course) is the student's responsibility

## Student Code of Conduct

- **Policy website:** [csuchico.edu/studentconduct](https://csuchico.edu/studentconduct)

## Grade Appeals

If you believe a final grade was assigned incorrectly or unfairly, you have the right to appeal.

### Grade Appeal Process
1. **Talk to your instructor first** — Many grade disputes are resolved informally at this stage
2. **Contact the Department Chair** if the issue is not resolved with the instructor
3. **File a formal appeal** — Must be submitted within **30 calendar days** of the date the grade is posted on your record
4. **Grade Appeal Committee** — Department or college-level committee reviews the appeal
5. **Dean's review** — If still unresolved, appeal escalates to the college dean

### Grounds for Appeal
- Arithmetic or clerical error in grade calculation
- Grading based on criteria not stated in the course syllabus
- Evidence of bias or discrimination in grading
- Note: Disagreement with an instructor's subjective assessment is generally not sufficient grounds for appeal

## Drop and Withdrawal Policy

### Adding and Dropping Courses
- **First two weeks of the semester:** Students may add or drop courses freely through the student portal (no penalty)
- **After week 2:** A "W" (Withdrawal) grade will appear on the transcript for dropped courses
- **Weeks 3–12:** Withdrawal requires **instructor signature** (or online approval in some departments)
- **After week 12:** Late withdrawals are only approved for serious and compelling reasons (medical, emergency); requires petition and dean's approval

### Withdrawal Grades
- A "W" grade does **not** affect GPA but does appear on the transcript
- Excessive withdrawals may affect financial aid Satisfactory Academic Progress (SAP)
- International students: Dropping below full-time enrollment (12 units) without authorization may affect visa status — contact International Students & Scholars before withdrawing

### Medical Withdrawal
- Students facing serious medical or mental health issues may petition for a Medical Withdrawal
- Applies to full withdrawal from all courses mid-semester
- Requires documentation from a healthcare provider
- Contact the Registrar's Office (SSC 140) for the petition form

## Incomplete Grade Policy

- An "I" (Incomplete) grade may be assigned when a student is unable to complete coursework due to unforeseen circumstances beyond their control (illness, family emergency, etc.)
- **Agreement Required:** Student and instructor must complete a formal **Incomplete Grade Agreement** form before the end of the semester
- **Completion Deadline:** The work must be completed within **one calendar year** of the end of the semester in which the "I" was assigned
- If not completed within one year, the "I" automatically converts to an "IC" (Incomplete Charged), which counts as an "F" in GPA calculation
- Incompletes are not appropriate for students who simply did not complete work without a compelling reason

## FERPA — Student Records Privacy

The **Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA)** protects the privacy of student education records.

### Key Rights
- Students have the right to **inspect and review** their own education records
- Students have the right to request amendment of inaccurate records
- Students have the right to control disclosure of their records to third parties

### Disclosure Rules
- CSU Chico cannot release personally identifiable student information to third parties (including parents) without the student's **written consent**
- Exception: Directory information (name, major, enrollment status) may be released unless the student opts out
- Exception: Certain disclosures are allowed without consent (e.g., school officials with legitimate interest, health/safety emergencies, judicial orders)

### To Authorize Release to Parents or Others
- Submit a **FERPA Consent to Release** form to the Registrar's Office (SSC 140)
- This authorization remains in effect until revoked in writing

## ADA / Section 504 Accommodation Policy

- CSU Chico is committed to providing reasonable accommodations to students with disabilities under the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act
- Students must be registered with the **Accessibility Resource Center (ARC)** to receive official accommodations
- **Faculty responsibility:** Faculty must implement approved accommodations within **5 business days** of receiving the Faculty Notification Letter from ARC
- Refusal to implement accommodations is a violation of federal law and university policy
- Students experiencing non-compliance should contact ARC at (530) 898-5959 immediately
- See the full Accessibility Resource Center guide for registration details

## Smoke-Free and Tobacco-Free Campus Policy

- **All CSU campuses** are entirely smoke-free and tobacco-free
- This policy applies to all campus property, including parking lots, outdoor areas, and university vehicles
- **Prohibited items include:** cigarettes, cigars, pipes, smokeless tobacco, e-cigarettes, vaping devices, and hookah
- Smoking is not permitted within any distance of buildings — the entire campus is a no-smoking zone
- Violations may result in a fine and referral to Student Conduct
- Resources for tobacco cessation available through the Student Health Center

## Service Animals and Emotional Support Animals

### Service Animals
- **Service animals** (as defined by the ADA) are permitted in **all campus buildings and areas**
- A service animal is a dog (or miniature horse) individually trained to perform specific tasks for a person with a disability
- University personnel may only ask two questions: (1) Is this a service animal required for a disability? (2) What work or task has the animal been trained to perform?
- Service animal owners are responsible for the animal's care, behavior, and any damage caused

### Emotional Support Animals (ESAs)
- ESAs provide comfort but are not trained to perform specific disability-related tasks
- ESAs are **not covered by ADA** and are **not permitted in academic buildings or public campus spaces**
- ESAs **are permitted in campus housing** with prior approval from University Housing
- To request ESA approval in housing:
  1. Register with the Accessibility Resource Center (ARC)
  2. Provide documentation from a licensed mental health professional
  3. Submit an ESA housing request to University Housing: housing@csuchico.edu
  4. Approval is granted on a case-by-case basis and is not guaranteed
