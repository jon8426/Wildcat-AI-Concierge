# Accessibility Resource Center (ARC)

## Overview

The Accessibility Resource Center (ARC) at CSU Chico is dedicated to providing equal access and opportunity to students with disabilities. ARC works collaboratively with students, faculty, and staff to ensure an inclusive educational environment in compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act.

## Who We Serve

ARC provides services to students with:
- Physical and mobility disabilities
- Visual impairments and blindness
- Hearing impairments and deafness
- Learning disabilities (e.g., dyslexia, ADHD)
- Psychological and psychiatric disabilities
- Chronic health conditions and medical disabilities
- Temporary disabilities (e.g., broken limb, surgery recovery)
- Traumatic brain injuries
- Autism spectrum disorder

## Services Provided

### Academic Accommodations
- **Extended time on exams** – typically 1.5x or 2x standard time
- **Reduced-distraction testing environment** – quiet testing rooms available at the ARC Testing Center
- **Note-taking assistance** – peer note-takers or audio recording permission
- **Preferential seating** – front-of-class or accessible seating arrangements
- **Alternate format materials** – textbooks and course materials in accessible formats (e.g., e-text, large print, Braille)
- **Flexible attendance policies** – coordinated with faculty for disability-related absences
- **Assistive technology** – screen readers, magnification software, speech-to-text tools available

### Communication Access
- **ASL (American Sign Language) interpreters** – available for classes, meetings, events, and campus activities
- **CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation)** – live captioning by a certified captioner
- **TypeWell** – meaning-for-meaning transcription for deaf and hard-of-hearing students
- **Captioned media** – assistance coordinating captioned versions of course materials and videos

### Mobility & Physical Access
- **Campus escort service** – available for students with mobility impairments; call (530) 898-5959 to arrange
- **Accessible parking** – Blue Zone spaces in every campus lot; ADA placard or Blue permit required
- **Priority registration** – students with disabilities may register for classes before general enrollment opens
- **Temporary disability services** – short-term accommodations for injuries, surgery, or illness

### Assistive Technology Lab
- Located within the ARC office (Student Services Center 170)
- Equipment available: screen readers (JAWS, NVDA), Dragon NaturallySpeaking, ZoomText, Kurzweil 3000
- Scanning and conversion services for alternate format materials
- Training on assistive technology available by appointment

## How to Register with ARC

Registering with ARC is a straightforward process. Students are encouraged to register as early as possible — ideally before the semester begins.

### Step-by-Step Registration Process

**Step 1: Submit Documentation**
- Provide documentation of your disability from a qualified professional (e.g., physician, psychologist, audiologist)
- Documentation should describe the nature of the disability, functional limitations, and recommended accommodations
- Submit documentation via email to arc@csuchico.edu or in person at SSC 170
- Documentation guidelines available at csuchico.edu/arc/documentation

**Step 2: Schedule an Intake Appointment**
- Call (530) 898-5959 or email arc@csuchico.edu to schedule an intake meeting with an ARC counselor
- Intake appointments typically last 45–60 minutes
- The counselor will review your documentation and discuss how your disability affects your academic experience

**Step 3: Receive Your Accommodation Letter**
- After the intake appointment, ARC will issue an official **Faculty Notification Letter** outlining your approved accommodations
- This letter is sent electronically to each of your professors at the start of every semester
- You must be registered with ARC each semester for letters to be distributed
- Students are encouraged to meet with their professors early in the semester to discuss accommodations

**Step 4: Utilize Your Accommodations**
- Work directly with your professors to implement accommodations
- Contact ARC if any issues arise with accommodation implementation
- Faculty are required to implement approved accommodations within **5 business days** of receiving the notification letter

## ASL Interpreter Requests

- **Advance notice required:** Minimum **2 weeks preferred** for classes and recurring events
- **Rush requests:** May be accommodated but cannot be guaranteed
- **How to request:** Email arc@csuchico.edu with:
  - Event/class name and location
  - Date, time, and duration
  - Contact information
- ARC maintains a pool of qualified interpreters and coordinates all assignments
- Interpreter services are provided at **no cost** to eligible registered students
- Interpreters are also available for campus events, meetings with faculty, and advising appointments

## Captioning Services

### CART (Communication Access Realtime Translation)
- Real-time stenographic captioning provided by a certified CART provider
- Captions displayed on a laptop or screen visible to the student
- Available for classes, campus events, and meetings
- Request via arc@csuchico.edu with at least 2 weeks' notice

### TypeWell
- Meaning-for-meaning transcription (condensed, accurate representation of speech)
- Suitable for lectures, labs, and discussions
- Transcript provided to student after each session
- Less resource-intensive than CART; good option when verbatim captioning is not required

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to re-register with ARC each semester?**
No. Once registered, your documentation remains on file. However, you must request your Faculty Notification Letters each semester through the ARC portal or by contacting the office.

**What if my professor refuses to implement my accommodations?**
Contact ARC immediately at (530) 898-5959. ARC will work with the faculty member and, if necessary, escalate to the appropriate academic dean.

**Can I request accommodations for a temporary condition like a broken arm?**
Yes. ARC provides short-term accommodations for temporary disabilities. A doctor's note describing the condition and expected duration is typically sufficient documentation.

**Is my disability information kept confidential?**
Yes. Disability information is protected under FERPA and ADA privacy regulations. Faculty receive accommodation letters that list approved accommodations without disclosing your specific diagnosis.
