# CSU Chico Parking Guide

## Overview

CSU Chico offers a variety of parking options for students, faculty, staff, and visitors. Permits are required to park on campus during enforcement hours. Planning ahead and purchasing permits online is strongly recommended to avoid citations.

---

## Parking Services Contact Information

- **Office Location:** Kendall Hall 130
- **Phone:** (530) 898-5475
- **Email:** parking@csuchico.edu
- **Online Permits & Info:** [csuchico.edu/parking](https://csuchico.edu/parking)
- **Office Hours:** Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM

---

## Enforcement Hours

- **Monday–Friday:** 7:00 AM – 10:00 PM
- Weekends and holidays: No enforcement (permits not required)
- Special event enforcement may apply outside normal hours

---

## Visitor Parking

Visitors to campus may park in designated visitor areas without a permit by using pay stations.

### Visitor Lots
- **Lot P7** – Visitor pay station area, central campus
- **Lot P8** – Visitor pay station area, near Kendall Hall
- **Lot P10** – Visitor pay station area, east side of campus

### Pay Station Rates
- **$2.00 per hour**
- Pay stations accept credit/debit cards and coins
- Maximum daily rate may apply; check posted signage
- Daily permits can also be purchased at pay stations for $8.00

---

## Student Permits

Students may purchase permits for the academic year, a single semester, or on a daily basis.

| Permit Type | Duration | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Permit | Full Academic Year | $180 |
| Semester Permit | One Semester | $120 |
| Daily Permit | One Day | $8 |

- Daily permits available at pay stations throughout campus or online at csuchico.edu/parking
- Student permits are designated for **Red Zone** lots (see Permit Zones below)
- Carpool permits available at reduced rates — contact Parking Services for details
- Permits are non-transferable and must display on the vehicle's rearview mirror or dashboard

---

## Faculty & Staff Permits

- **Permit Type:** Zone A (Gold permit)
- **Primary Lots:** Lot 22 (near academic core), Lot 23 (near Kendall Hall)
- Faculty/Staff permits allow parking in all Gold-designated zones
- Annual payroll deduction option available through HR
- Contact Parking Services for departmental bulk permit arrangements

---

## Permit Types & Zones

| Permit Color | Who It's For | Authorized Zones |
|---|---|---|
| **Red** | Student commuters | Red-designated student lots |
| **Gold** | Faculty & Staff | Gold-designated lots (Lot 22, 23, and others) |
| **Blue** | ADA/Disability | All blue ADA-marked spaces campus-wide |
| **Motorcycle** | Motorcycles/scooters | Designated motorcycle zones |

- All permits must be registered to a specific license plate via the online portal
- Lost or stolen permits should be reported to Parking Services immediately

---

## ADA Accessible Parking

- **Blue Zone** spaces are available in every campus parking lot
- A valid state-issued ADA placard or license plate is required
- Blue permit from Parking Services is also available for students/employees with disabilities
- ADA spaces are closest to building entrances in each lot
- Contact the Accessibility Resource Center (ARC) at (530) 898-5959 for additional assistance
- ADA enforcement is in effect 24/7, including weekends

---

## Electric Vehicle (EV) Charging

EV charging stations are available at two campus locations:

- **Lot 11** – Multiple Level 2 charging stations
- **Lot 27** – Multiple Level 2 charging stations

- A valid parking permit is required while using EV charging spaces
- Charging time limits posted at each station
- Visit csuchico.edu/parking for current charging station availability and network information

---

## Purchasing & Managing Permits Online

1. Go to [csuchico.edu/parking](https://csuchico.edu/parking)
2. Log in with your CSU Chico portal credentials
3. Select "Purchase Permit" and choose your permit type
4. Enter your vehicle's license plate number
5. Complete payment — credit/debit cards accepted
6. Permits are linked to your license plate (no physical hang tag required for new permits)

- Virtual permits are activated immediately upon purchase
- Permit history and vehicle registration managed through the online portal

---

## Citations & Appeals

- Citations are issued for expired permits, incorrect zones, blocking fire lanes, and other violations
- **Fine amounts** vary by violation type; check the citation or csuchico.edu/parking for the current fee schedule
- **Appeals:** Must be filed within **21 calendar days** of citation issuance
- Submit appeals online through the Parking Services portal or in person at Kendall Hall 130
- Unpaid citations may result in a hold on student records

---

## Additional Tips

- Arrive early during the first weeks of each semester — lots fill quickly
- Semester permit prices are pro-rated for late purchases in some cases
- Bicycle parking is available throughout campus as a permit-free alternative (see Campus Map)
- Campus shuttle service is free for students with a valid student ID
